## The benchmarks (verified)
- **Gross margin:** B2B SaaS median **79%**, top-quartile **85%** (Benchmarkit) — but **local-first pushes Rambo to ~85–90%+** because most compute runs on the user's machine and enterprise self-hosts.
- **Retention:** bootstrapped SaaS median **NRR 103% / GRR 91%** (SaaS Capital).
- **Conversion:** dev-tool freemium realistically converts **1–5%** (plan for the low end); credit-card trials convert higher but yield *fewer* total customers (ChartMogul).
- **CAC:** ~**$1.76** of S&M per $1 new ARR (2023, Benchmarkit) — which is why GTM is **bottom-up/free-led**, not paid.

## The key insight
**Sub-$5K-ACV products struggle to reach 100% NRR.** Combined with 1–5% dev-tool freemium conversion, a cheap per-seat PLG tool is a *churn grind*. So the free/cheap tiers exist to **acquire**, and the business **compounds** on the higher-ACV **enterprise/self-hosted** tier. The numbers, not just intuition, point to the enterprise endgame.

## Illustrative path to ~$1M ARR
A small-team-sustainable mix:
- ~1,000 enterprise self-hosted seats @ ~$35/mo ≈ **$420K/yr**
- ~3,000 team seats @ ~$12/mo ≈ **$430K/yr**
- individual/one-off ≈ **$100K** *(lumpy — treat as bonus)*

≈ **$850K recurring + one-off.**

## The honest shape
This is a **capital-efficient, profitable $1–10M-ARR business** — not a unicorn. The real-world reference is **Bruno**: ~14 people sustained on ~200K MAU with **<2% paying**. The risk isn't margin (local-first keeps it high) — it's the **conversion grind**, which is why low burn + patience + the enterprise lever are the plan.
