## The ladder

| Tier | Model | Target price | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | local-first; free for up to *X* members | **$0** | the adoption engine |
| **Team** | per-seat annual + cloud-consumption meter | **~$8–12 /seat/mo** | vs Postman $19; Obsidian $4 · AFFiNE $10 · Bruno $6–11 |
| **Enterprise** | annual per-seat license + support/SLA | **~$30–40 /seat/mo** | vs Postman $49 · Insomnia $45 |

Free-for-*X*-members is a direct counter to **Postman removing free team collaboration (March 2026)**.

## The strategy
**Undercut Postman on price *and* features.** Be cheaper at every paid tier, and give the free/cheap tiers *more* (free small-team collaboration, all protocols, the agent surface, LLM testing) than the incumbents do. The margin still works because the product is local-first (minimal infra) — see [unit economics](/rambo/business/unit-economics/).

:::note[Where usage-based pricing belongs]
Metering is on **backend-synced resources only** — seats, synced collections, cloud runs — never on local artifacts you create on your own machine. See [metering](/rambo/business/metering/).
:::
