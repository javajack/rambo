:::caution[Read this honestly]
All market-size figures below are **secondary, syndicated market-research estimates** — opaque methodology, frequently revised, PR-distributed. Treat them as **directional**, not precise. The important move is separating two markets vendors blur.
:::

## Two markets, not one

| Segment | Size | What it is | Relevance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **API *management*** (gateways, lifecycle) | **$8.86B (2025) → $22.1B (2031)**, ~16% CAGR (Mordor) | Kong / Apigee / AWS territory | **Not our market** — a TAM mirage |
| **API *testing*** (full) | **~$4.0B–$8.24B by 2030** (analyst spread) | functionality/perf/security testing | Adjacent |
| **API testing *tools/software*** | **~$2.6B by 2030**, ~15% CAGR | the actual product slice | **Our realistic SAM** |

The analyst spread on the testing market is real and driven by base-year/scope differences (TechSci sizes it at $4.15B→$8.24B by 2030; Data Bridge and Research-and-Markets cluster nearer ~$4B). The **tools/software** sub-slice — the truest proxy for an API client — is roughly two-thirds of the low-end total, **~$2.6B by 2030**.

## The incumbent's scale (the moat to respect)

- **Postman:** self-reports **35M+ users, 500K orgs, 98% of the Fortune 500** (Oct 2024, unaudited). Last reported valuation **$5.6B** (2021); **~$2.8B** secondary-market estimate (2026).
- **Kong (Insomnia's owner):** **$100M ARR** (Dec 2023, primarily its Konnect gateway, **not** Insomnia); **$2B** Series E valuation (Nov 2024). Insomnia has no broken-out revenue.

## What this means

A bootstrapped entrant does **not** win a ~$2.6B SAM against a 35M-user incumbent. The goal is a **profitable, defensible niche** — a $1–10M-ARR business that compounds via the enterprise/self-hosted tier. The market is big enough for that many times over, and the incumbent's de-rating + trust missteps create the opening. See [unit economics](/rambo/business/unit-economics/) for the model.
