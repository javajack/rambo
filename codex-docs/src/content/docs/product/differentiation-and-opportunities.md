## Build-ranked opportunities

| Rank | Opportunity | Evidence strength | Why it can matter |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Local-first no-account trust contract | High | Directly answers forced account/cloud lock-in pain |
| 2 | High-fidelity import/migration | High | Converts pain into switching; migration friction protects incumbents |
| 3 | Secrets local-by-default with encrypted sharing | High | Trust-critical and enterprise-relevant |
| 4 | LLM/SSE stream debugger | Medium-high | Specific unserved pain; not just generic AI |
| 5 | Agent-safe privacy layer | High as threat response, Medium-high as wedge | RESTK validates schema extraction, synthetic data, redaction, and AI audit as concrete agent workflow needs |
| 6 | Deterministic local CLI + permissioned MCP | Medium-high | Agent workflows need reproducible local control, not only hosted MCP |
| 7 | Large-response performance | High | Daily workflow pain; visible quality differentiator |
| 8 | Reviewable sync/merge | Medium | Supports git/local-first and team paid tier |
| 9 | Enterprise storage control/self-host | Medium-high | Needed for high-ACV buyers, but later sales proof required |
| 10 | Request-adjacent network diagnostics | Medium-high for diagnostics, Medium for product wedge | DNS/TCP/TLS/timing checks reduce API failure ambiguity, but should stay request-centric |

## Weak or not-yet-build opportunities

| Idea | Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| AI request authoring as primary wedge | Weakened | Incumbents already market AI authoring; hard to defend |
| Generic MCP support | Weakened | Postman, Yaak, RESTK, and ApiArk already have or claim MCP/agent surfaces |
| Local-first/Git-native as standalone wedge | Weakened | Bruno, Yaak, ApiArk, Voiden, and LiteClient all converge on local/file/Git/no-login claims |
| Contract testing suite | Not validated | Useful, but not proven as API-client switching wedge |
| Governance linting | Not validated | Enterprise-adjacent, but buyer urgency unclear for a new client |
| Response diffing | Upgraded to plausible v1.5 | ApiArk and Postmate both position response diff/compare as product value; still weaker than import/secrets/streaming |
| Live co-editing/presence | Weak | Collaboration demand is more about safe sharing/review than presence |
| General-purpose network toolbox | Weakened | DNS/TCP/TLS/timing is validated; broad ping/traceroute/WHOIS/LAN scanner scope is not |

## Differentiation standard

Promote a feature only if it satisfies at least two:

- validated recurring pain;
- hard for cloud-first incumbents to copy without model conflict;
- directly improves switching;
- creates paid team/enterprise expansion;
- makes agent/LLM workflows materially more reliable;
- remains differentiable after comparing against ApiArk, RESTK, Voiden, Yaak, LiteClient, and other new entrants.
